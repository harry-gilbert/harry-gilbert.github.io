Dolby Laboratories, Inc.

Student Placement — Broadcast Group

1yr (+1.5mth ext)

In my third year at DMU, I undertook a placement year with Dolby Laboratories, Inc. which I thoroughly enjoyed, leading to me extending my contract with them before returning to Uni.

My main role was to assist the Broadcast team in delivering Dolby Atmos to the European broadcast market while also supporting the existing deployment of Dolby technologies in the field.

    I did this, in part, by:
  • Diagramming digital audio/video processing workflows for different events (including a major worldwide sporting event)
  • Producing a proof-of-concept website to aid the visualisation and understanding of a project
  • Testing professional and consumer broadcast equipment (sometimes before shipping out on loan)
  • Demo/test content creation
  • Representing Dolby in working with professional partners and the wider industry

This role taught me a huge amount and gave me confidence in myself to be able to work with such a big company and have such responsibilities.

The Incidents

Drummer, Sound Engineer, Editor

7yrs

The Incidents were an indie rock band from Northamptonshire with music published to iTunes, Spotify and Amazon Music. We also had appearances on The Beat, many BBC radio stations across the UK, HFM and Corby FM.

I was the drummer for its entirety, sound engineer and co-producer behind the ‘Iceberg | Complex | Forced Entry | Joni’ EP, and director and editor of the ‘Little America’ and ‘Rose Gold’ Official Music Videos.

Being part of this group kickstarted almost every professional interest that I have now. Recording music in many professional studios made me curious how the whole audio production workflow functioned. When we needed graphic art for an album cover I got to work learning how to create digital art. Bringing a camera to photograph our recording sessions evolved into directing and editing two official music videos.
